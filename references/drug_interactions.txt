DRUG-DRUG INTERACTIONS — CLINICAL SAFETY REFERENCE
Source: DrugBank Open Data (CC BY 4.0) + DDInter Database
Severity Classification: SEVERE | MODERATE | MILD
Last Updated: 2024

SEVERITY DEFINITIONS:
  SEVERE   — Life-threatening or permanent damage possible.
             Combination generally contraindicated.
  MODERATE — Significant risk requiring dose adjustment,
             enhanced monitoring, or timing separation.
  MILD     — Minor clinical effect. Usually manageable.

SEVERE INTERACTIONS — CONTRAINDICATED OR USE ONLY WITH EXTREME CAUTION
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

INTERACTION 001: WARFARIN + IBUPROFEN (or any NSAID)
Severity: SEVERE
Mechanism: Dual mechanism — (1) NSAIDs inhibit platelet aggregation,
impairing primary haemostasis. (2) NSAIDs (especially ibuprofen)
competitively displace warfarin from plasma protein binding sites
(albumin), increasing free warfarin concentration and INR.
Additionally, NSAIDs cause gastric mucosal damage, increasing GI
bleed risk independent of coagulation status.
Clinical Effect: Significantly elevated INR, GI haemorrhage,
intracranial bleeding. Fatal outcomes documented.
Management: AVOID concurrent use. If unavoidable (short course
only, under supervision): use lowest effective NSAID dose,
add proton pump inhibitor, monitor INR every 1–2 days.
Alternative analgesic: Paracetamol (acetaminophen) is the
preferred analgesic for patients on warfarin — minimal effect
on INR at standard doses (up to 2g/day). Note: High-dose
paracetamol (>2g/day) can modestly increase INR — monitor.

INTERACTION 002: MORPHINE + BENZODIAZEPINES
Severity: SEVERE
Drugs involved: Morphine, oxycodone, codeine, tramadol, fentanyl
+ Diazepam, lorazepam, alprazolam, clonazepam, temazepam
Mechanism: Additive and synergistic CNS and respiratory depression.
Both drug classes suppress brainstem respiratory centres through
independent but additive pathways (mu-opioid receptors and
GABA-A receptors respectively).
Clinical Effect: Profound respiratory depression, apnoea,
loss of consciousness, coma, death. Risk markedly increased
in opioid-naive patients and with IV/IM administration.
FDA Black Box Warning: Issued 2016 for concurrent opioid +
benzodiazepine use.
Management: Avoid concurrent use where possible. If combined,
use lowest effective doses, limit duration, ensure naloxone
available, educate patient and caregivers on warning signs.
Increased monitoring required.

INTERACTION 003: ACE INHIBITOR + POTASSIUM SUPPLEMENTS
Severity: SEVERE
Drugs involved: Enalapril, lisinopril, ramipril, perindopril,
captopril + Potassium chloride supplements, potassium-sparing
diuretics (spironolactone, eplerenone, amiloride, triamterene)
Mechanism: ACE inhibitors block angiotensin II, reducing
aldosterone secretion, which normally promotes renal potassium
excretion. Adding potassium supplements or potassium-sparing
diuretics creates additive hyperkalemic effect — serum potassium
can rise to dangerous levels (>6.0 mmol/L).
Clinical Effect: Hyperkalaemia — muscle weakness, paralysis,
cardiac conduction abnormalities, ventricular fibrillation,
cardiac arrest. Risk highest in elderly, diabetic, and patients
with renal impairment.
Management: Avoid routine combination without clear indication.
If combination necessary (e.g. spironolactone in heart failure):
Monitor serum potassium and eGFR at baseline, 1 week, 4 weeks,
then every 3–6 months. Target K+ < 5.5 mmol/L.
Stop potassium supplement if K+ > 5.5 mmol/L.

INTERACTION 004: METFORMIN + IODINATED IV CONTRAST MEDIA
Severity: SEVERE (conditional)
Mechanism: Iodinated contrast agents can cause acute kidney injury.
In patients with renal impairment, contrast-induced AKI reduces
metformin clearance, causing accumulation and potentially fatal
lactic acidosis.
Clinical Effect: Lactic acidosis — potentially fatal.
Symptoms: nausea, vomiting, abdominal pain, hyperventilation,
altered consciousness, cardiovascular collapse.
Management Protocol:
  eGFR ≥ 60: No action required for most contrast studies.
  eGFR 45–60: Hold metformin at time of procedure, restart
  48 hours later only if renal function stable.
  eGFR 30–45: Hold metformin 48 hours BEFORE and AFTER contrast.
  eGFR < 30: Metformin contraindicated (not a contrast issue —
  metformin is absolutely contraindicated at this eGFR level).

INTERACTION 005: ATENOLOL (BETA-BLOCKER) + VERAPAMIL / DILTIAZEM
Severity: SEVERE
Mechanism: Both drug classes depress cardiac conduction and
contractility through different mechanisms. Beta-blockers reduce
sympathetic drive to the SA and AV nodes. Non-dihydropyridine
calcium channel blockers (verapamil, diltiazem) directly suppress
SA and AV node conduction.
Clinical Effect: Severe bradycardia, complete heart block
(third degree AV block), asystole, acute heart failure.
Risk highest with IV administration of either agent.
Management: Oral combination requires cardiology supervision
and ECG monitoring. IV combination is CONTRAINDICATED.
Note: This interaction does NOT apply to dihydropyridine calcium
channel blockers (amlodipine, nifedipine, felodipine) — those
do not significantly affect cardiac conduction.

MODERATE INTERACTIONS — REQUIRE MONITORING OR DOSE ADJUSTMENT
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

INTERACTION 006: AMLODIPINE + SIMVASTATIN
Severity: MODERATE
Mechanism: Amlodipine inhibits CYP3A4, the primary enzyme
responsible for simvastatin metabolism. This increases simvastatin
plasma levels significantly.
Clinical Effect: Myopathy, rhabdomyolysis (breakdown of muscle
tissue causing acute kidney injury and dark urine).
Management: Do not exceed simvastatin 20 mg/day when combined
with amlodipine. Consider switching to pravastatin or rosuvastatin
(not significantly metabolised by CYP3A4).

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

INTERACTION 007: AMOXICILLIN + WARFARIN
Severity: MODERATE
Mechanism: Amoxicillin reduces gut flora that produce
Vitamin K2, lowering available vitamin K and potentiating
warfarin's anticoagulant effect. Additionally, infections
themselves often raise INR.
Clinical Effect: Elevated INR, increased bleeding risk.
Effect typically seen 3–7 days into antibiotic course.
Management: Increase INR monitoring frequency during
and for 1 week after antibiotic course. Anticipate possible
warfarin dose reduction.

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

INTERACTION 008: METFORMIN + ALCOHOL
Severity: MODERATE
Mechanism: Chronic heavy alcohol use impairs hepatic lactate
metabolism and can cause hepatic insufficiency, both of which
increase lactic acidosis risk with metformin. Alcohol also
potentiates the hypoglycaemic effect of metformin.
Clinical Effect: Increased lactic acidosis risk with heavy
regular drinking. Hypoglycaemia risk.
Management: Advise patients to avoid heavy alcohol consumption.
Occasional moderate drinking (1–2 units) is generally acceptable.
Binge drinking is contraindicated.

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

INTERACTION 009: IBUPROFEN + ACE INHIBITOR + DIURETIC
Severity: SEVERE (triple combination)
This is known clinically as the "TRIPLE WHAMMY" interaction.
Mechanism: NSAIDs reduce renal prostaglandin synthesis, causing
renal vasoconstriction. ACE inhibitors reduce efferent
arteriolar tone. Diuretics cause volume depletion. The three
mechanisms together severely compromise renal perfusion.
Clinical Effect: Acute kidney injury. Most dangerous in elderly,
volume-depleted, and diabetic patients. Can cause irreversible
renal failure requiring dialysis.
Management: Avoid this three-drug combination entirely.
If NSAID required in a patient on ACEi + diuretic: consider
holding diuretic, ensure adequate hydration, monitor creatinine
within 1 week.

SAFE ALTERNATIVES REFERENCE
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

SAFE ANALGESIC FOR ANTICOAGULATED PATIENTS:
  Preferred: Paracetamol (acetaminophen) up to 2g/day
  Acceptable: Codeine (with caution — constipation, limited efficacy)
  Avoid: All NSAIDs (ibuprofen, naproxen, diclofenac, aspirin)
  Avoid: High-dose aspirin (low-dose 75mg antiplatelet use is
  separate clinical decision made by prescribing physician)

SAFE STATIN WITH AMLODIPINE:
  Preferred: Pravastatin (not CYP3A4 metabolised)
  Preferred: Rosuvastatin (minimal CYP3A4 interaction)
  Caution: Simvastatin — max 20mg/day
  Avoid: Simvastatin > 20mg/day

PREFERRED ANTIHYPERTENSIVE COMBINATIONS:
  ACEi + Calcium channel blocker (amlodipine): SAFE and recommended
  ACEi + Thiazide diuretic: SAFE and recommended
  ACEi + Potassium-sparing diuretic: CAUTION — monitor K+
  ACEi + Potassium supplement: CAUTION — monitor K+, avoid routine
