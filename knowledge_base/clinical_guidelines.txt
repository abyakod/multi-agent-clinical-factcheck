CLINICAL TREATMENT GUIDELINES — PRIMARY CARE REFERENCE
Source: WHO, CDC, NHS NICE Guidelines (Public Domain)
Scope: Hypertension, Type 2 Diabetes, Pain Management, Anticoagulation
Last Updated: 2024

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
GUIDELINE 1: HYPERTENSION MANAGEMENT
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

DIAGNOSIS THRESHOLDS (2023 WHO/ISH Guidelines):
  Normal BP:          < 120/80 mmHg
  Elevated BP:        120–129 / < 80 mmHg
  Stage 1 Hypertension: 130–139 / 80–89 mmHg
  Stage 2 Hypertension: ≥ 140 / ≥ 90 mmHg
  Hypertensive crisis:  > 180 / > 120 mmHg (emergency)

TREATMENT INITIATION:
  Stage 1 without CVD risk: Lifestyle modification first.
  Pharmacotherapy if BP persists > 130/80 after 3 months.
  Stage 2: Initiate pharmacotherapy at diagnosis alongside
  lifestyle changes.
  Diabetes + hypertension: Target < 130/80 mmHg.
  CKD + hypertension: Target < 130/80 mmHg.
  Elderly (≥ 65 years): Target 130–140 / 70–90 mmHg.
  Caution with aggressive lowering in elderly — risk of falls.

FIRST-LINE PHARMACOTHERAPY (WHO preferred):
  1. ACE inhibitor (enalapril, lisinopril, ramipril)
     — Preferred in diabetes with proteinuria, post-MI,
       heart failure, CKD (renal protective)
  2. ARB (losartan, valsartan)
     — Use when ACEi not tolerated (ACEi cough)
  3. Calcium channel blocker (amlodipine, felodipine)
     — Preferred in elderly, African descent, isolated
       systolic hypertension
  4. Thiazide diuretic (hydrochlorothiazide, indapamide)
     — Preferred in sodium-sensitive hypertension,
       heart failure with fluid overload

COMBINATION THERAPY:
  Two agents often required. Preferred combinations:
    ACEi/ARB + CCB (most evidence — reduces CV events)
    ACEi/ARB + Thiazide
    CCB + Thiazide
  Avoid: ACEi + ARB (dual renin-angiotensin blockade —
  increased AKI and hyperkalaemia without additional
  BP benefit. Not recommended.)
  Avoid: Beta-blocker as first-line unless additional
  indication (angina, post-MI, heart failure, arrhythmia).

LIFESTYLE MODIFICATIONS (all stages):
  - Sodium restriction: < 2g/day (< 5g salt/day)
  - DASH diet: Fruits, vegetables, low-fat dairy, reduced saturated fat
  - Physical activity: 150 minutes moderate intensity/week
  - Weight reduction: 5–10 mmHg per 5 kg weight loss
  - Alcohol limitation: < 2 units/day (men), < 1 unit/day (women)
  - Smoking cessation: Does not lower BP but reduces CV risk

MONITORING:
  After initiation: BP check at 1 month. Electrolytes and
  eGFR at 1–4 weeks if on ACEi/ARB or diuretic.
  Stable hypertension: 3–6 monthly reviews.
  Annual: Fasting lipids, HbA1c, eGFR, urine albumin:creatinine ratio.

GUIDELINE 2: TYPE 2 DIABETES MANAGEMENT
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

DIAGNOSIS CRITERIA (WHO 2023):
  HbA1c ≥ 48 mmol/mol (6.5%) on two occasions
  Fasting plasma glucose ≥ 7.0 mmol/L (126 mg/dL)
  2-hour plasma glucose ≥ 11.1 mmol/L during OGTT
  Random plasma glucose ≥ 11.1 mmol/L with symptoms

GLYCAEMIC TARGETS:
  General population: HbA1c < 53 mmol/mol (7.0%)
  Elderly / frail / high hypoglycaemia risk: < 64 mmol/mol (8.0%)
  Cardiovascular disease present: < 53 mmol/mol (7.0%)
  Pregnancy with diabetes: < 48 mmol/mol (6.5%) — strict

PHARMACOTHERAPY — STEP APPROACH:
  Step 1 (First-line — all patients without contraindication):
    METFORMIN — start at diagnosis alongside lifestyle
    Titrate to maximum tolerated dose over 4–8 weeks
    Target: HbA1c reduction of 1.5–2.0% (16–22 mmol/mol)

  Step 2 (if HbA1c not at target after 3 months on metformin):
    Add one of:
    - SGLT-2 inhibitor (empagliflozin, dapagliflozin)
      Preferred in: established CVD, heart failure, CKD
    - GLP-1 receptor agonist (semaglutide, liraglutide)
      Preferred in: obesity, CVD risk reduction needed
    - DPP-4 inhibitor (sitagliptin, saxagliptin)
      Preferred in: elderly, renal impairment, low hypoglycaemia risk
    - Sulphonylurea (glibenclamide, glipizide)
      Low cost option but carries hypoglycaemia risk

  Step 3 (triple therapy or insulin):
    Add third agent OR initiate insulin if:
    HbA1c > 75 mmol/mol (9.0%), symptomatic hyperglycaemia,
    or weight loss > 10% (consider insulin)

INSULIN INITIATION IN TYPE 2:
  Indication: HbA1c persistently > 75 mmol/mol despite
  dual/triple oral therapy, OR symptomatic hyperglycaemia.
  Starting dose: 10 units bedtime basal insulin, or
  0.1–0.2 units/kg/day.
  Titration: Increase by 2 units every 3 days until
  fasting glucose 4–7 mmol/L.
  Continue metformin with insulin (reduces insulin dose
  requirement and weight gain).

SICK DAY RULES:
  During acute illness with vomiting/dehydration, HOLD:
  - Metformin (lactic acidosis risk when dehydrated)
  - SGLT-2 inhibitors (euglycaemic DKA risk)
  - Sulphonylureas (hypoglycaemia risk with reduced intake)
  Continue: Insulin (reduce dose, increase monitoring)
  Ensure adequate hydration. Monitor glucose 2–4 hourly.

GUIDELINE 3: PAIN MANAGEMENT FRAMEWORK
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

WHO ANALGESIC LADDER (3-step):
  Step 1 — Mild Pain (NRS 1–3):
    Non-opioid: Paracetamol 500 mg–1 g every 4–6 hours
    Maximum: 4 g/day (3 g/day in elderly, hepatic impairment,
    or regular alcohol consumption > 2 units/day)
    Add: NSAID if inflammation present AND no contraindication
    (note contraindications: anticoagulants, renal impairment,
    peptic ulcer, pregnancy, cardiovascular risk)

  Step 2 — Moderate Pain (NRS 4–6):
    Weak opioid: Codeine 30–60 mg every 4–6 hours
    Or: Tramadol 50–100 mg every 4–6 hours
    Continue: Paracetamol (combination provides additive effect)
    Add: Adjuvant if neuropathic component (amitriptyline,
    gabapentin, pregabalin)

  Step 3 — Severe Pain (NRS 7–10):
    Strong opioid: Morphine, oxycodone, hydromorphone
    Morphine: 5–15 mg immediate-release every 4 hours
    Titrate to effect — no ceiling dose for cancer pain
    Continue: Non-opioid adjuvants
    Add: Regular laxative (opioids cause constipation — universal)
    Anti-emetic initially (nausea common in first 1–2 weeks)

ANALGESIC SELECTION FOR SPECIAL POPULATIONS:

  ANTICOAGULATED PATIENTS (on warfarin, heparin, DOAC):
    FIRST CHOICE: Paracetamol up to 2 g/day
    ACCEPTABLE: Low-dose codeine with monitoring
    CONTRAINDICATED: ALL NSAIDs — ibuprofen, naproxen,
    diclofenac, aspirin, ketorolac, celecoxib, etoricoxib
    Rationale: NSAIDs increase bleeding risk through platelet
    inhibition AND may alter anticoagulant drug levels.

  RENAL IMPAIRMENT (eGFR < 30 mL/min):
    SAFE: Paracetamol (preferred)
    AVOID: NSAIDs (worsen renal function)
    AVOID: Morphine (metabolite accumulation)
    USE WITH CAUTION: Fentanyl, hydromorphone (better
    pharmacokinetic profile in renal impairment)

  ELDERLY PATIENTS (≥ 75 years):
    Start with lowest effective dose of any analgesic.
    Paracetamol: Maximum 3 g/day.
    NSAIDs: Avoid if possible — higher GI bleed, renal,
    and cardiovascular risk in elderly.
    Opioids: Start low, go slow — increased sensitivity,
    fall risk, constipation, cognitive effects.

  PREGNANCY:
    First/Second trimester: Paracetamol acceptable.
    NSAIDs: Avoid from 20 weeks — premature ductus closure.
    NSAIDs: Absolutely contraindicated from 30 weeks onward.
    Opioids: Only under specialist supervision — neonatal
    withdrawal syndrome if used near term.

GUIDELINE 4: ANTICOAGULATION MANAGEMENT
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

INDICATIONS FOR ANTICOAGULATION:
  Atrial fibrillation (CHA₂DS₂-VASc score ≥ 2 in men, ≥ 3 in women)
  Deep vein thrombosis (DVT)
  Pulmonary embolism (PE)
  Mechanical heart valves (warfarin ONLY — DOACs not indicated)
  Antiphospholipid syndrome

ANTICOAGULANT OPTIONS:
  WARFARIN:
    INR target 2.0–3.0 (most indications)
    INR target 2.5–3.5 (mechanical mitral valve, high-risk valves)
    Monitoring: Minimum monthly when stable, more frequent
    if dose change, illness, or new medication
    Reversal: Vitamin K (slow — 6–12 hours),
    4-factor PCC (fast — for life-threatening bleeding)

  DIRECT ORAL ANTICOAGULANTS (DOACs):
    Apixaban, rivaroxaban, dabigatran, edoxaban
    No routine INR monitoring required
    NOT suitable for: Mechanical heart valves,
    antiphospholipid syndrome (use warfarin)
    Require dose reduction in renal impairment
    Reversal agents: Idarucizumab (dabigatran),
    Andexanet alfa (apixaban, rivaroxaban)

IMPORTANT: ANALGESIC CHOICE IN ANTICOAGULATED PATIENTS:
  The ONLY safe first-line analgesic for patients on ANY
  anticoagulant (warfarin, DOAC, heparin) is PARACETAMOL.
  NSAIDs are contraindicated in anticoagulated patients
  regardless of INR level, anticoagulant type, or NSAID dose.
  This is an absolute clinical rule — not a precaution.
